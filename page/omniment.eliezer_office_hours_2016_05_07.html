Hedons

Q: Let's review the writer-reader feedback cycle

  • Nate comes to Arbital with a topic he wants to write about. He follows the How to author on Arbital! guide and produces a series of pages he's happy with.
  • All of the pages show up on the front page, and a few readers dive in.
  • Should only one of them show up -- should Nate promote some page as the "entry point" into his explanation?
  • Nate's pages get some likes, and remain on the front page.
  • should pages decay off the front page?
  • Nate checks out his user page, sees that he's gotten L likes and taught N people some concepts. He decides to write some more.

Q: How do updates like "L people liked your thing" and "N people learned from your page appear"? What about updates like "5 people were confused by this sentence"?

Q: Is XP part of the MFP for the beta?
